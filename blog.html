How Pingu Shop Started

By Cakey • April 2026

Every small shop begins with something very un-glamorous: a random idea that refuses to leave.

Pingu Shop started the same way. It wasn’t some grand business plan or a sudden burst of genius. It was just a quiet love for small, cute things, the kind that make ordinary days feel a little softer. Sticky notes, tiny notebooks, pens that glide too smoothly for their own good… things that don’t change your life, but somehow make it better.

At first, it was just collecting. Then sharing. Then came the moment where “maybe I should turn this into something real” stopped sounding ridiculous.

Of course, it wasn’t easy. There were doubts, awkward beginnings, and that constant question: “Is anyone even going to care?” But slowly, piece by piece, Pingu Shop became more than just a collection of products. It became a space where people could find small joys, the kind you don’t really need, but somehow can’t resist.

And honestly, that’s the whole point. Not everything has to be practical. Sometimes, it just has to make you smile.



Top 5 Cute Stationery Items

By Strawberry • September 2025

There are two types of people: those who buy stationery to use… and those who buy it because it’s too cute to touch. If you’re here, you already know which one you are. Here are 5 stationery items that somehow make studying feel less painful:

1. Pastel Highlighters They do the exact same job as normal highlighters. But suddenly your notes look like they belong to someone who has their life together.

2. Mini Notebooks Too small for serious work, too cute to ignore. Perfect for random thoughts, to-do lists, or pretending you’re organized.

3. Character Stickers Zero academic value. Maximum emotional support.

4. Gel Pens Smooth ink, satisfying writing, and just enough aesthetic to make you want to write one more line.

5. Sticky Notes You’ll buy them for productivity and end up using them to decorate everything instead.

At the end of the day, cute stationery doesn’t magically make you more productive. But it makes the process a little more enjoyable… and sometimes, that’s enough to keep going.
